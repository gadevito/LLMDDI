than uniform positive bias, we analyzed GPT-4o's prediction confidence via
log-probabilities on the Validation. Correct predictions exhibited
significantly higher confidence (mean logprob: 0.99, SD: 0.07)
than incorrect predictions (mean: 0.89, SD: 0.22);
Mann-Whitney U test, p < 0.001, effect size r = +0.58
(large effect).

Critically, true positive predictions showed higher confidence
(mean logprob: 0.98) than false positive predictions
(mean: 0.87), with the difference
being statistically significant (Mann-Whitney U, p < 0.001,
r = +0.32, medium effect). This calibration
pattern indicates the model assigns high confidence selectively to genuine
interactions rather than indiscriminately to all positive predictions.
